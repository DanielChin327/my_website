Pajeon Recipe

Pajeon, also known as Korean green onion pancake, is a savory dish made primarily with green onions, flour, and an egg-based batter. This web page will guide you through the simple steps to prepare this delicious and crispy Korean appetizer, perfect for a quick meal or a tasty snack. Pajeon is commonly enjoyed with a tangy soy dipping sauce that complements its flavors beautifully.

Preparation Time

  • Prep Time: 15 Minutes
  • Cook Time: 15 Minutes
  • Total Time: 30 Minutes

Ingredients (For 2 People)

  • 1 bunch of green onions
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup cold water
  • 1 egg
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • Vegetable oil for frying
  • Optional: seafood (shrimp, squid) or vegetables (carrot, zucchini) for added texture and flavor

Dipping Sauce (Note: Just Soy Sauce is also fine)

  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon sesame seeds
  • 1 chopped green onion (optional)
  • 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Green Onions: Wash the green onions and trim the roots. Cut them into 4-inch lengths.
  2. Make the Batter: In a mixing bowl, combine the flour, cold water, egg, and salt. Mix until smooth.
  3. Add Ingredients: Add the green onions to the batter, making sure they are well-coated. If using additional ingredients like seafood or vegetables, add them to the batter now.
  4. Heat the Pan: Heat a non-stick skillet over medium-high heat and add a generous amount of vegetable oil.
  5. Cook the Pancake: Pour the batter with green onions into the skillet, spreading it out evenly. Press down with a spatula to flatten. Cook until the bottom is golden brown and crispy, about 3-4 minutes.
  6. Flip the Pancake: Carefully flip the pancake and cook the other side until golden brown and crispy, another 3-4 minutes.
  7. Prepare the Dipping Sauce: While the pancake is cooking, mix all the dipping sauce ingredients in a small bowl.
  8. Serve: Cut the green onion jeon into bite-sized pieces and serve hot with the dipping sauce.
